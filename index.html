A three-tier distribution network across Eastern Indonesia, modelled as a mixed-integer linear program to compress the logistics cost stack.

Tugas Besar · TI2201 Operations Research I · Semester 4 · Kelompok 10 · 5 contributors. Real industry case: Marx and Engels AG, a German FMCG operator in Indonesia.
// PROJ · A01 · Lead TI2201 · Penelitian Operasional I · Sem. 4 · 2025 Completed

Distribution Network Optimisation for Marx & Engels AG across Kalimantan, Sulawesi, and Balinusra.

Baseline FY2023 transport cost ran 14× the internal estimate. The brief: rebuild the network from first principles and prove a lower-cost configuration.

Industry case set by the TI2201 faculty. Marx and Engels AG operates a complex Indonesia-wide FMCG distribution network from a single SDC in Karawang into Eastern Indonesia: Kalimantan, Sulawesi, and the Bali-Nusa Tenggara corridor. We mapped 58 logistics nodes (1 SDC, 15 Direct DC/CPU, 12 Indirect DC, 30 Depo) and two distribution legs: first-mile (port-to-DC via container shipping), and middle-mile (DC-to-DC and DC-to-Depo via CDD, CDE, GrandMax, Kijang).

The model is a Mixed Integer Linear Program adapted from a CVRPTW reference (Praditya, 2019), extended to a multi-echelon cost structure with four decision-variable families: warehouse supply allocation, first-mile trip count by container type, middle-mile vehicle count by type and route, and driver headcount. The objective minimises the full cost stack: variable trip cost, fixed container and ocean-freight cost, middle-mile fuel cost (distance / efficiency × price), and fixed driver and warehouse-staff cost.

Methods applied MILP formulation · Fishbone (Ishikawa) root-cause analysis · Influence diagram for cost-driver mapping · Fixed-vs-variable cost decomposition · Capacity and demand balance constraints · Integer-trip enforcement.
Headline diagnostics
  • 14×FY2023 actual transport cost over the internal USD 2.6 M middle-mile estimate.
  • 86.6kMonthly box demand in Kalimantan against 52.2k supply, a 34.4k shortfall.
  • 557First-mile container trips per month, fragmented across 20FT, 40FT, FUSO, CDD, Build Up.
  • 58Network nodes modelled across three regions and four facility tiers.

// Industrial relevance

Why this generalises beyond FMCG.

Network design · fleet sizing

Any multi-plant manufacturer with a customer or distribution network faces the same problem shape: first-mile raw-material in, middle-mile inter-plant or to OEM, last-mile to customer. The MILP structure used here (variable + fixed cost layers, integer trip variables, capacity-and-demand balance) is the standard template for network-design and fleet-sizing problems. The reusable artefact is the formulation itself: swap parameters, keep the model.

// PROJ · A02 TI3202 · Supply Chain & Resource Planning · Sem. 5 · 2025 Completed

Full PPIC Cycle for PT Volta Energi Nusantara, an EV battery manufacturer.

End-to-end supply chain study on a 6-month, 2-material demand dataset. The deliverable walked the complete PPIC loop (demand forecasting, inventory policy, multi-tier DRP, supplier selection, and warehouse sizing) and closed with a 160 m² warehouse recommendation backed by a working inventory policy.
Methods applied Time-Series Decomposition (multiplicative) for seasonal demand · EOQ with safety stock and ROP at 95 % service level · DRP cascade across three distribution tiers · AHP supplier scoring on four criteria (lead-time weight 0.503) · Suprayogi warehouse sizing formula.
Key findings
  • +Multiplicative decomposition materially outperformed naive forecasts on MAPE.
  • +EOQ analysis recommended a 23 % cut in ordering frequency.
  • +AHP selected PT Andalan (Mat. A) and PT Plasindo (Mat. B).
  • +Warehouse sized at 160 m² via Suprayogi sizing.
// PROJ · A03 TI3205 · Facility Layout Planning · Sem. 5 · 2025 Completed

Factory & Office Layout Automation for PT TriX Group (Group C13).

Designed and automated a full factory floor plan and office layout in Microsoft Visio via VBA macros. The macro reads a parametric input file and generates room coordinates, aisle placements, door logic, and furniture templates. The factory module derives department adjacency through an Activity Area Diagram and emits the placement back into Visio.
Methods applied Systematic Layout Planning (SLP) · AAD adjacency matrix with A/E/I/O/U/X classification · VBA shape generation for Visio · Aisle and door-logic programming · Multi-room coordinate calculation.
Key findings
  • +Full factory floor plan generated programmatically from an adjacency-matrix input.
  • +Office layout macro places 15 room types with door and aisle logic.
  • +Reduced manual Visio drawing time by ~80 % via automation.
  • +SLP adjacency approach directly portable to injection-molding plant layout.
